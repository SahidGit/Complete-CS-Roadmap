## 📄 Resume & ATS Optimization

To secure initial screening rounds, your resume must be structured for both human recruiters and automated systems (ATS).

### ATS-Friendly Guidelines
Applicant Tracking Systems parse text to match candidates against job descriptions. If the system fails to read your layout, your resume will be automatically filtered out.

1. **Layout & Formatting**:
   - Use a **single-column layout** only. Multi-column structures frequently scramble text order during parsing.
   - Use standard margins (0.5 to 1 inch) and clean page partitions.
   - Avoid tables, text boxes, graphics, icons, progress bars, or charts. These look like garbage data to a parser.
   - Save your resume in **PDF** format (most standard) or **DOCX** unless the application instructions explicitly specify otherwise.
2. **Typography & Styling**:
   - Stick to universal, web-safe fonts: **Arial**, **Calibri**, **Helvetica**, or **Georgia** (avoid fancy custom typefaces).
   - Use standard header titles: `Experience` or `Work Experience`, `Projects` or `Personal Projects`, `Education`, and `Skills`. Parsers fail when sections are given creative names like "My Journey" or "Core Competencies".
3. **Keyword Optimization**:
   - Carefully review the target Job Description (JD). Extract hard skills and technologies (e.g. *TypeScript*, *REST APIs*, *State Management*) and weave them naturally into your bullet points.

### Resume Writing Tips
- **The XYZ Formula (Google standard)**: Focus on impact. Write your bullet points as: *"Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]"*.
  - *Weak:* "Created a weather application in React."
  - *Strong:* "Built a responsive weather client in React, decreasing API latency by 35% through custom hooks caching."
- **Action Verbs**: Start every bullet point with strong action verbs: *Engineered, Spearheaded, Developed, Optimized, Architected, Refactored*.
- **Keep it Concise**: Keep the resume strictly to **1 page** if you have under 5 years of experience.

## 🔗 LinkedIn Profile Optimization

A polished LinkedIn profile serves as a passive pipeline for inbound recruiter opportunities.

### 1. Header & Title (The Hook)
Your headline determines whether search algorithms index your profile.
- **Formula**: `Target Role | Core Stack | Key Achievement or Value Proposition`
  - *Example:* `Frontend Engineer | React, TypeScript, Next.js | Open Source Contributor`
- **Banner and Photo**: Use a clean, professional headshot with good lighting. Create a custom background banner using tools like Canva, matching your stack or design philosophy.

### 2. About Summary (The Pitch)
Write in the first person. Structure it into three short paragraphs:
- **Who you are**: A passionate developer specializing in building performance-driven web products.
- **What you build**: Mention your core stack (React, Node, etc.) and your primary focus areas (e.g., performance tuning, accessible UIs, scalable APIs).
- **Call to action**: State that you are open to opportunities and provide a direct contact method (e.g. `"Reach out at email@domain.com"`).

### 3. Experience & Projects
- Treat your LinkedIn experience section similar to your resume. Use structured bullet points detailing outcomes rather than listing simple responsibilities.
- Link your primary projects to their live URLs and code repositories.
- Complete the **Skills** section (add at least 15 relevant technical tags) and pin your top 3 core skills (e.g. *JavaScript*, *React*, *CSS*).
